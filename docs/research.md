## 3. Core Concepts from the Webinar

### The "Comfortable Illusion" Framing
This is the webinar's central provocation: most companies have AI strategies that are "an internally focused collection of use cases aimed at efficiency gains, cost reduction, or incremental product improvement" — while missing the more fundamental question of how AI is reshaping the business model itself.

The danger is optimizing the current operating model while the underlying economics of the market are changing. Organizations remain comfortable in their existing frame while the competitive landscape shifts beneath them.

**Source (verbatim from BOI webinar page):**
> "AI is changing industry economics — shifting where value is created, who captures it, and what remains defensible. It is compressing margins, lowering barriers to entry, and reshaping customer expectations, fast. Yet many organizations remain internally focused, optimizing their existing operating model while the underlying logic of the market is changing."

### Three Tracks / Mechanisms of AI Disruption
From Laura's blog "AI is shifting power, margins and control":
1. **Commoditization of outputs** — AI doesn't replace products but makes formerly premium outputs (e.g., professional assessments, reports) table stakes. Margins compress.
2. **Interface takeover** — AI doesn't replace the product but takes over the interface through which decisions are made. Examples: AI-powered search replacing Google clicks, AI agents replacing human-browsed funnels.
3. **Structural asymmetry** — AI-native organizations deliver the output of large professional services teams with a fraction of the headcount. They scale faster, adapt continuously, and can reconfigure workflows in near real time.

*Note: The webinar's exact "three tracks" framing may differ slightly — this is inferred from the closely related blog content written by Laura. The webinar description promises "a clear set of transformation moves."*

### Three Waves of AI Adoption (BOI Framework)
From Philippe De Ridder's blog, used across BOI content:
- **Wave 1:** Apply AI to existing ways of working — time savings, cost reduction, efficiency gains
- **Wave 2:** Leverage AI for better quality and enhanced outcomes — rethinking how existing systems work
- **Wave 3:** Create entirely new systems and redefine markets — new business models that couldn't exist without AI

Key insight: "Success in the first wave doesn't guarantee success in the third." BOI advocates a portfolio approach across all three waves.

**Source:** https://www.boardofinnovation.com/blog/the-three-waves-of-ai-adoption/

### Agentic Commerce
A major theme in BOI's current thinking:

**Definition:** The era where AI agents, not humans, make the choices that used to fill the marketing funnel. Discovery, consideration, and conversion happen inside AI systems, not human-browsed interfaces.

**Key stat (cited by BOI):** 83% of searches that trigger Google's AI Overviews now end without a single click. Traditional funnels don't just underperform — they vanish.
**Source:** Similarweb data, cited in BOI webinar page and corroborated by click-vision.com analysis.
